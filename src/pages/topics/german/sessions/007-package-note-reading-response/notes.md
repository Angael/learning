## Source note

`Hallo Marta, ich bin Lena aus der Nachbarwohnung. Der Paketbote war heute hier. Ich habe das Paket für dich angenommen, weil du nicht zu Hause warst. Es liegt jetzt bei mir. Du kannst es heute ab 18 Uhr oder morgen ab 8 Uhr abholen. Viele Grüße, Lena`

Whitespace-token count: 47.

## Exact prompt

1. Write one compact Polish line in the form `who has it now; why she accepted it; earliest collection time`.
2. Write exactly one German thanks using `Danke, dass ... angenommen hast.`
3. Write exactly one German collection confirmation using `Ich hole es heute/morgen um ... Uhr ab.`

Do not add a question, explanation, or third German sentence.

## Expected reasoning

- Lena, the neighbour, has the parcel now. The courier no longer has it.
- Lena accepted it because Marta was not at home.
- `heute ab 18 Uhr` is earlier than `morgen ab 8 Uhr`; the earliest collection is today at 18:00.
- Both source-text uses of `es` refer to neuter `das Paket`.
- After `dass`, the supplied Perfekt elements close the clause as `... angenommen hast`.
- In the main clause, separable `abholen` gives `Ich hole ... ab`.
- A collection sentence must select a time inside one stated range: today at 18:00 or later, or tomorrow at 08:00 or later.

## Expected answer

A. `Lena; ponieważ Marty nie było w domu; dziś od 18:00.`

B1. `Danke, dass du das Paket angenommen hast.`

B2. `Ich hole es heute um 18 Uhr ab.`

## Acceptable variants

- The Polish line may use a full sentence or short labelled fragments if all three facts are clear.
- `Lena, sąsiadka` or `sąsiadka Lena` is acceptable for the first fact. `Kurier` alone is not.
- `bo Marta była poza domem` and equivalent concise Polish wording are acceptable for the reason.
- B1 may use `Danke, dass du es angenommen hast.` because the parcel referent is clear. The taught default keeps `das Paket` visible.
- `Vielen Dank, dass ...` is acceptable.
- B2 may choose today at 18:00 or any clearly later time, or tomorrow at 08:00 or any clearly later time. A time before today's 18:00 is not supported.
- `Ich hole das Paket ... ab.` is acceptable in B2, though the supplied frame uses `es`.
- `ae/oe/ue/ss` substitutions are accepted. Minor punctuation, capitalization, or spelling slips are corrected inline when meaning and target order remain clear.
- Do not infer independent Perfekt or open practical fluency: the vocabulary, verb form, word-order frame, pronoun referent, and time ranges are supplied.

## Likely errors and next hint

- If the learner names the courier, ask who has the parcel `jetzt` after accepting it.
- If the learner chooses tomorrow 08:00 as earliest, compare `heute` with `morgen` before comparing clock numbers.
- If the learner writes `dass du hast ... angenommen` or `dass du ... hast angenommen`, point to the model and ask which two words close the `dass` clause.
- If the learner writes `Ich abhole ...`, point to the supplied main-clause frame `Ich hole ... ab.`
