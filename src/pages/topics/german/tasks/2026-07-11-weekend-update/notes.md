## Expected reasoning

- Finite verb ends subordinate clause after `weil`, `dass`, and `ob`.
- Fronted subordinate clause occupies first position; main-clause finite verb follows comma: `Weil ..., bin ich ...`, not `Weil ..., ich bin ...`.
- In Perfekt, auxiliary is finite verb: `weil ich viel gearbeitet habe`; participle is not finite.
- `ob` means whether, not conditional if.

## Grading

1. Connector meaning and subordinate-clause finite-verb position.
2. Inversion after fronted subordinate clause.
3. Perfekt auxiliary and participle.
4. Required 6–8 sentences, connectors, two completed actions, finite-verb check.
5. Communicative clarity.
6. Only then high-value spelling/article corrections. Accept `ae/oe/ue/ss`.

Secure: all forms present; subordinate clauses verb-final; fronted clause triggers inversion; finite verbs identified; update understandable.

Developing: complete understandable update with one or two word-order errors repairable after finite verbs are marked.

## Likely misconception

- Main-clause V2 retained inside subordinate clause.
- `Weil ..., ich bin ...` after fronted clause.
- Participle identified as finite instead of auxiliary.

## Next hint

Underline one finite verb per clause. Move subordinate finite verb to end. If subordinate clause comes first, put main-clause finite verb immediately after comma.
